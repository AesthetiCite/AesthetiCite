The IVFFlat issue is the most important thing here
You're right that cluster quality degrades as new documents are added without re-clustering. At 760K → 790K that's roughly a 4% increase — marginal degradation, but worth fixing given the HNSW migration is already planned.
The correct action after M4 closes is replace IVFFlat with HNSW entirely, not re-cluster IVFFlat. IVFFlat requires you to pick the number of clusters at build time and re-cluster when the corpus grows significantly. HNSW has no such requirement — new vectors are inserted directly into the graph structure. You never need to rebuild it when new documents are added.
The hnsw_migration.py delivered earlier handles this. Run it after M4 closes and the dedup pass completes, not before — you want to build the HNSW index on the final corpus, not rebuild it twice.
