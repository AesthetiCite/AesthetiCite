Here's the precise difference between what existed and what was built.

What existed before
The WORKFLOWS dict in clinical_decision.py had plain string steps — "Stop injection", "Massage area". The UI rendered them as a basic list with tap-to-complete. No timers, no drug blocks, no branch logic. It was served as part of the larger /api/decide response, not its own endpoint.
What was built
workflow_engine.py has 8 complete protocols. Each step has 7 fields: type, priority, action (the verb), detail, timer_seconds, drug/dose, and branch (conditional logic). The most important three:
Timers — Step 7 of vascular occlusion is "Monitor capillary refill every 5 min" with timer_seconds: 300. When the clinician reaches that step, a circular countdown starts automatically. At 0:00 it turns red. Step 8 is "No improvement at 60 min? Repeat hyaluronidase" with timer_seconds: 3600. Both run simultaneously — you're watching the 5-minute check interval while the 60-minute escalation window counts down in the background.
Branch logic — Step 2 of vascular occlusion checks for visual symptoms. If the condition is met, a red banner appears: "STOP this protocol. Call 999 immediately." This is not a footnote in text. It is a fullscreen interrupt. The clinician cannot proceed past it without acknowledging the condition is not met.
Drug/dose blocks — Every medication step shows the drug name and dose in a distinct highlighted block, separate from the explanation text. "Hyaluronidase · 1500 IU minimum — Maximum 10,000 IU total". The clinician doesn't have to parse it out of a paragraph.
Focus Mode — the top-right button switches to one-step-per-screen. The current action fills the entire view at 32px font. One verb. Impossible to misread. This is what you need when there is a patient in front of you with blanching and your hands are shaking.
The completion screen shows the LogCaseButton pre-filled with all the drugs from completed steps. Logging the case takes 10 seconds from the completion screen.
