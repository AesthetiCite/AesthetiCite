What each phase adds:
Phase A — 15 journals by ISSN. Pulls every paper from Aesthetic Surgery Journal, JAMA Dermatology, British Journal of Dermatology, Lasers in Medical Science, and 11 others — directly by ISSN, not keyword. This is the highest-yield phase because keyword searches miss papers whose titles don't contain your trigger terms. Expected yield: 40K–80K new documents.
Phase B — 42 specialty-gap queries. Topics structurally absent from M2/M4 — hyaluronidase pharmacology, facial artery anatomy, biofilm and late-onset nodules, wound healing, clinical governance, local anaesthetics, skincare actives, body contouring, PRP, thread lifts, scarring. These directly support AesthetiCite's clinical content. Expected yield: 30K–60K new documents.
Phase C — 26 multilingual queries. French, Spanish, Portuguese, German, Italian, Arabic — aesthetic medicine literature that exists on PubMed but was largely untouched by M2/M4. Expected yield: 10K–25K new documents.
Total projected: 80K–165K new documents, closing the gap to somewhere between 850K and 940K. Whether you reach 1M depends on duplication rates — the --dry-run flag on each phase tells you before you commit.
